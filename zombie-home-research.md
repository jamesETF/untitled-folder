## Strategy #1: Buy the Mortgage Note (Consensus Best Approach)

### How It Works
Identify the mortgage holder via ACRIS, contact the servicer, buy the non-performing note at 30-70 cents on the dollar, then either negotiate a deed-in-lieu or foreclose.

### Why It's Powerful
- You control the timeline — you decide when to foreclose
- You can "credit bid" at foreclosure sale (no additional cash needed)
- RPAPL 1309 allows expedited foreclosure for properties meeting the statutory definition of "abandoned" — potentially under 1 year
- Banks are motivated to sell because zombie property maintenance obligations (RPAPL 1308-1310) cost them $500/day in penalties

### Where to Find Notes
- Direct outreach to servicers (identify via MERS ServicerID lookup)
- Paperstac (online marketplace for mortgage notes)
- LoanMLS / FCI Exchange
- Note brokers
- FDIC/HUD sales (if original lender failed)

### Due Diligence on the Note
- **Original wet-ink promissory note** — does the seller have it, or just a lost note affidavit?
- **Chain of endorsements** — must be unbroken from originator through each holder
- **Chain of assignments in ACRIS** — every assignment should be recorded
- **MERS issues** — confirm MERS properly assigned the mortgage
- **Statute of limitations** — CRITICAL: The Foreclosure Abuse Prevention Act (FAPA, Dec 2022) eliminated de-acceleration. If the mortgage was accelerated 6+ years ago, the note may be time-barred and unenforceable.
- **Collateral file** — original note, mortgage, allonges, assignments, title policy, appraisal

### Post-Acquisition Options
1. **Deed-in-lieu** (if borrower/heirs located): Borrower signs over property in exchange for debt release. Fastest path: 2-4 months.
2. **Foreclosure**: Judicial process in NY Supreme Court. Service by publication if borrower unlocatable. Credit bid at referee's sale. Timeline: 18-36 months uncontested, 3-7+ years if complicated.

### Devil's Advocate Warnings
- NYC foreclosure averages 1,910 days (5.2 years) — longest in the nation
- CPLR 3408 mandatory settlement conferences apply even if borrower doesn't appear
- HETPA (expanded July 2024) may classify transactions as "covered contracts" with rescission rights and treble damages
- New deed theft criminal law (July 2024) — Class B felony, up to 25 years. First indictments Aug 2025.

## Strategy #2: Find and Buy from Heirs (Surrogate's Court Path)

### How It Works
If the owner is deceased, search Surrogate's Court for probate filings. If no estate was opened, you (as a tax lien creditor) can petition for letters of administration. Then negotiate a purchase from the estate or foreclose on your lien.

### Key Tools
- ACRIS for the deed + owner identity
- Social Security Death Index to confirm death
- Surrogate's Court records for probate filings
- Public Administrator (each NYC county has one) — can sell estate property with court approval
- Professional skip tracing services for locating heirs

### The Public Administrator Path
- Each NYC county has a Public Administrator responsible for administering estates of decedents who die without known heirs
- The PA's office is overwhelmed and under-resourced — properties languish for years
- Interested parties can petition the Surrogate to compel the PA to act
- The PA can sell estate property with court approval and convey marketable title

### Devil's Advocate Warnings
- Unknown heirs can appear years later and challenge proceedings
- The amended Partition Act blocks investors who buy fractional heir interests from forcing a partition sale
- Surrogate's Court proceedings take 6-24 months
- EPTL 4-1.1 gives broad inheritance rights to distant relatives (cousins, half-siblings, etc.)
- If no heirs exist, property escheats to NY State — not to you

## Strategy #3: Tax Lien Acquisition + Foreclosure

### How It Works
NYC sells delinquent tax liens annually to the NYCTL trust. Buy individual liens on the secondary market from the trust servicer, wait 2+ years, then foreclose judicially. Tax liens are senior to mortgages — foreclosure can extinguish the mortgage.

### Key Mechanics
- NYC bundles thousands of liens and sells them to the NYCTL trust (not individual auction)
- Interest rate: typically 18% per annum
- Redemption period: owner can redeem by paying lien + interest + penalties
- Foreclosure eligibility: after 2+ years from sale date
- Total timeline from lien purchase to deed: 3-6 years realistically

### How to Acquire Liens on Specific Properties
- Contact the NYCTL trust servicer about purchasing individual liens
- Buy on the secondary market (lien certificates are assignable)
- Monitor the DOF website for annual lien sale lists

### Devil's Advocate Warnings
- You buy a LIEN, not the property — converting to ownership requires full judicial foreclosure
- NYC doesn't sell to individuals at auction (bulk trust sales only)
- Owner redemption rights are extensive; city actively helps owners avoid losing properties
- Federal tax liens survive unless IRS got proper 25-day notice
- Environmental contamination liability transfers to new owner under CERCLA
- City Council passed bills Dec 2025 to abolish lien sales by 2029 (Mayor vetoed, override pending)
- The city's own accumulated liens (HPD repairs, water, ECB fines) may exceed property value

## Strategy #4: City Programs (HPD/TPT)

### Third Party Transfer (TPT) Program
- HPD transfers severely distressed properties to qualified developers (primarily nonprofits)
- Properties transferred for nominal consideration (often $1/building)
- Requires commitment to rehabilitate as affordable housing
- Regulatory agreements impose 30-40 year affordability restrictions
- Program was frozen 2019; being revived under Int. 1063 with strict guardrails

### Neighborhood Entrepreneur Program (NEP)
- HPD sells city-owned 1-4 family homes to qualified purchasers
- Below-market prices
- Must commit to occupying or renting at affordable rates

### HDFC Pathway
- City-owned buildings transferred to Housing Development Fund Corporations
- Limited-equity co-ops with affordability restrictions
- Often $250/unit from the city

### Joint Venture with Nonprofits
- Partner with 501(c)(3) for tax exemptions (RPTL 420-a)
- Combine with LIHTC (4% credit + tax-exempt bonds) for rehabilitation
- Nonprofit must genuinely control property — IRS scrutinizes these arrangements

### Devil's Advocate Warning
The city has no interest in feeding distressed housing to private speculators. Every program comes with strings: affordability covenants, rehabilitation requirements, resale restrictions, ongoing regulatory oversight.

## Strategy #5: Adverse Possession (Last Resort)

### Legal Requirements (Post-2008 RPAPL Amendments)
1. Continuous possession for 10 years
2. Open and notorious
3. Hostile and under claim of right — must have "reasonable basis for belief" property is yours
4. Exclusive
5. Actual possession

### Why It Almost Never Works
- The 2008 amendments require a "reasonable basis" for believing the property is yours — knowing occupants of someone else's abandoned building fail this test
- Courts are hostile to whole-parcel claims in urban areas (vs. boundary disputes)
- Success rate: fewer than 5% for entire NYC parcels
- Title insurance companies refuse to insure (even after court judgment, many underwriters decline)
- 10-year timeline is easily interrupted by any owner/heir/city action
- Government land is immune from adverse possession entirely

### When It Could Work
- Occupation began before July 7, 2008 (pre-amendment common law standard applies)
- Long-standing boundary encroachments with improvements (fences, driveways, gardens)
- Strong documentary evidence of continuous investment and maintenance

## The Hidden Cost Stack

The reason these properties are abandoned — the previous owner did the math:

- **Back taxes + penalties**: $20K-$100K+
- **Water/sewer arrears**: $10K-$100K+
- **ECB/DOB violation fines**: $10K-$200K+
- **HPD emergency repair liens**: Variable, often inflated by prevailing wage requirements
- **Lead paint abatement** (pre-1978 buildings): $50K-$200K+
- **Asbestos removal**: $50K-$200K+
- **Structural rehabilitation** (burst pipes, roof collapse, mold, foundation): $50K-$500K+
- **Legal fees across all strategies**: $30K-$60K minimum
- **Carrying costs** (insurance, maintenance, utilities during 3-7 year process): $10K-$30K/year

**Rule of thumb:** Total all-in cost must be 60-70% of after-repair value (ARV) to justify the risk.

## 2024-2026 Policy Shifts Working Against Private Acquisition

1. **Deed Theft Criminal Law** (July 2024) — Class B felony (up to 25 years). First indictments Aug 2025. AG has concurrent jurisdiction.
2. **HETPA Expansion** (July 2024) — now covers properties with utility liens. 5-day rescission, treble damages, 6-year SOL.
3. **Foreclosure Abuse Prevention Act** (Dec 2022) — eliminated de-acceleration of mortgages, potentially rendering old accelerated notes unenforceable.
4. **Tax Lien Sale Abolition** — Council passed bills Dec 2025 to end lien sales by 2029. Direction: city-controlled land bank.
5. **TPT Reform** (Int. 1063) — revived program designed for community/nonprofit ownership, not private investors.
6. **Zombie Property Penalties** — $500/day on lenders who fail to maintain. HPD crossed $1M in penalties as of 2022.

## Step-by-Step Playbook

### Weeks 1-2: Due Diligence
Full title search and lien analysis via ACRIS, DOF, DOB, HPD, ECB, DEP, Surrogate's Court. Build the complete lien stack. Phase I environmental. Run the numbers.

### Weeks 2-4: Find the People
Skip trace the owner. Alive? Dead? Heirs? Contact the mortgage servicer (MERS lookup). Check if HPD has the property on any action list.

### Then Pick Your Path

| Situation | Strategy |
|---|---|
| Owner alive, findable | Direct purchase negotiation |
| Owner deceased, heirs exist | Buy from heirs (may need to open estate in Surrogate's Court) |
| Owner deceased, no heirs | Buy the note + foreclose, or work with Public Administrator |
| No mortgage / mortgage time-barred | Tax lien path |
| City already pursuing in rem | Apply through HPD programs (likely need nonprofit partner) |

### Entity Structure
- Single-purpose LLC (never personal name)
- Isolates environmental, structural, and tort liability
- NY LLC or Delaware LLC registered in NY

### Financing
- Traditional bank financing generally unavailable for zombie acquisition
- Private/hard money lenders, private equity, or self-funding
- Refinance into conventional after acquisition and renovation
- CDFIs may lend for affordable housing rehabilitation

### Title Insurance
- Standard insurers often decline; work with specialty title companies
- Quiet title judgment (RPAPL Article 15) is the gold standard for insurability
- Budget $20-40K for quiet title action, 6-18 months timeline
- Indemnity agreements or escrow holdbacks may be required
